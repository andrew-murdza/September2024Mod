AOE Mod Behavior Overview
=========================

Purpose of this file
--------------------

This file is an audit-oriented summary of what the mod currently appears to do based on the Java source, JSON data, assets, recipes, tags, worldgen files, and mixin configuration in the project. It is intentionally organized by gameplay area rather than by file so it is easier to check whether each behavior is wanted.

This is not a player-facing guide. It is meant to help identify:

- Features the mod is supposed to have and whether they are represented in code/data.
- Behavior-changing mixins that may have wide side effects.
- New blocks, items, mobs, worldgen, recipes, dimensions, and integrations.
- Things the mod currently does that might not be intended.


1. High-level world and dimension changes
-----------------------------------------

The mod replaces the vanilla Overworld dimension configuration through data/minecraft/dimension/overworld.json. The Overworld uses the custom dimension type aoemod:aoedim and the custom noise settings aoemod:aoedim.

The custom dimension type aoemod:aoedim keeps many Overworld-like rules:

- Natural dimension.
- Skylight enabled.
- Beds work.
- Raids work.
- Minimum Y is -64.
- Height and logical height are 384.
- Ambient light is 0.1.
- Monster spawning is allowed from light level 0 through 7.
- Piglin-safe is set to true.
- Respawn anchors do not work.

The custom noise settings aoemod:aoedim use custom density functions for continents, rivers, offsets, and terrain shaping. Aquifers are disabled, sea level is 64, the default block is stone, the default fluid is water, and ore veins are disabled.

The mod registers custom density functions:

- aoemod:continents creates large repeating biome/continent bands.
- aoemod:rivers creates repeating radial/vertical river cuts based heavily on chunk X position.
- aoemod:offset creates biome/region-specific vertical offsets, including terrain stepping around transition areas.

The terrain is divided into custom bands/regions such as jungle, savanna, plains, mountains/grove, desert, deep dark, soul sand valley, warped forest, crimson forest, and basalt deltas. Several features and mob spawns depend on these biome bands.


2. Biomes and biome spawn themes
--------------------------------

The mod defines custom biome JSONs for the AOE dimension/Overworld replacement. Their spawns are grouped into difficulty and theme bands.

Jungle:

- Common monsters include creaking, slime, and spider.
- Water and ambient water life include dolphin, axolotl, turtle, end fish, salmon, squid, pufferfish, cod, lobster, tropical fish, and arrow squid.
- Creatures include panda, parrot, ocelot, cat, frog, gorilla, fox, wolf, rabbit, deer, chicken, and squirrel.

Savanna:

- Common monsters include zombie, skeleton, and Born in Chaos senor pumpkin.
- Water life includes squid, pufferfish, cod, lobster, tropical fish, salmon, and arrow squid.
- Creatures include horse, llama, donkey, rabbit, deer, chicken, cow, sheep, pig, squirrel, emu, bison, moose, gazelle, and boar.

Plains:

- Common monsters include pillager, ravager, and zombie villager.
- Water life includes salmon, squid, cod, and lobster.
- Creatures include horse, donkey, rabbit, deer, chicken, cow, sheep, pig, and squirrel.

Mountains / Grove:

- Common monsters include stray, breeze, and drowned.
- The grove also receives mooshrooms, with spawning restricted to mycelium.
- Water life includes dolphin, salmon, squid, lobster, arrow squid, cod, end fish, and glow squid.
- Creatures include fox, polar bear, mooshroom, rabbit, goat, and moose.

Desert:

- Common monsters include husk and parched.
- Rabbits and camels spawn as creatures.
- Water life includes glowdine, cubozoa, end fish, cod, glow squid, and searing cod depending on the configured water category.

Deep Dark:

- Common monsters include enderman and shulker.
- Glow squid and some custom fish behavior are configured around this region.

Soul Sand Valley:

- Common monsters include skeleton and Quark wraith.
- Nether cave/water-like creature groups include cubozoa.

Warped Forest:

- Common monsters include zoglin and creeper.
- Warped toads spawn as a creature group.

Crimson Forest:

- Hoglins spawn as a creature group.
- Nether fish spawn naturally in lava-like water ambient slots.

Basalt Deltas:

- Common monsters include blaze and magma cube.
- Striders spawn as a creature group.
- Lava/water ambient life includes glowdine, searing cod, and cubozoa.

The mod also uses custom mob caps/categories for some cave creature groups. Mushroom cave creatures are focused around mooshrooms, and nether cave creature groups include hoglins, striders, and warped toads.


3. Structures and cave systems
------------------------------

The mod adds large custom cave structures and pieces.

Long Nether Cave:

- Implemented through LongNetherCaveStructure and NetherCavePiece.
- Generates a long spiral/nether-themed cave system.
- Includes themed bands such as deep dark, soul sand valley, warped forest, crimson forest, and basalt deltas.
- Has custom floor, wall, ceiling, river/lava, and seafloor replacement logic.
- Replaces visible ores and gravel on cave floors/walls/ceilings with local base materials, and only replaces sand/sandstone on ceilings, so ores are not exposed from inside the cave.
- Local base materials include stone, deepslate, netherrack, soul sand, soul soil, blackstone, and basalt depending on region.
- Netherrack, stone, and deepslate replacement logic avoids overwriting special surface blocks such as crimson nylium, warped nylium, mycelium, sculk, and end stone.
- The seafloor above deep dark nether cave areas uses a sand layer with sandstone below it.

Long Mushroom Cave:

- Implemented through LongMushroomCaveStructure and MushroomCavePiece.
- Generates a large cave layer with mushroom, mycelium, grass, amethyst, calcite, smooth basalt, and water/floor features.
- Amethyst floor generation attempts to put budding amethyst only on the mathematical outer edge of the amethyst ellipsoid, with regular amethyst inside.
- Mushroom caves avoid carving into spruce leaves and spruce logs.
- Mushroom cave floor features use deterministic patch/grid placement so areas can have guaranteed distributions rather than purely random scattered features.

Important audit note:

- The mushroom cave budding-amethyst logic is deliberately mathematical. If it still creates too much or too little budding amethyst, the issue is probably in the ellipsoid boundary test or interaction between multiple overlapping carving ellipsoids.


4. Worldgen feature system
--------------------------

The mod registers several custom feature types:

- special_biome_tree
- seagrass
- kelp
- all_surface
- random_selector
- block_column
- glow_lichen
- huge_glow_shroom
- crimson_seagrass
- warped_seagrass
- grid_choice
- regional_grid_choice
- feature_sequence
- fixed_count_random_patch
- layered_all_surface
- rainforest_tree
- cave_vines
- sugar_cane
- chunk_patch

All-surface and layered all-surface features:

- Scan chunk surfaces for valid placement locations.
- Can target land, water, lava, or mixed surfaces depending on configuration.
- Support biome tags, placement chances, skip heights, and guaranteed placement behavior.
- Some guaranteed placement logic searches for the correct ground height at the target X/Z instead of using only the original feature origin.

Patch features:

- fixed_count_random_patch uses a shuffled finite list of candidate positions, skips water/lava for land patches, and stops after the valid candidate area has been checked.
- chunk_patch handles deterministic chunk-based patch placement, keeps rainforest feature origins anchored to chunk intersections, lets land patches spill around those intersections into clearings between center-of-chunk trees, uses shuffled finite candidate positions, skips water/lava for land patches, and uses a combined half-footprint on river shorelines so invalid water quadrants do not reduce shore density twice.

Grid features:

- grid_choice and regional_grid_choice select features using deterministic grids.
- These can enforce section quotas, pinned entries, weighted entries, and region-specific distributions.
- Regional floor grids keep trees centered when their JSON uses center offsets, allow corner-offset floor patches to spill from chunk x=0 and x=6 shorelines, and skip fully-river chunks between those shorelines.
- They are used for things like mushroom cave floor distributions, soul sand valley feature grids, jungle land patches, river/shoreline features, and rainforest/non-tree features.

Rainforest tree feature:

- Places template-based canopies using configurable log and leaves block state providers.
- Supports a 2x2 trunk centered on a defined origin grid.
- Supports top_y placement so a canopy can end at a specific Y value.
- Supports height providers when top_y is not used.
- Replaces template log/leaves with the configured providers.
- Preserves log orientation.
- Forces placed leaves to be non-persistent when the leaves block supports that property.
- Treats structure void as empty space and replaces air in templates with structure void where appropriate.
- Can place moss/coral around submerged 2x2 trunks.
- Coral columns are chosen vertically so color/type changes by height, with special top-color ordering.
- Underwater blocks around trunks become moss if they do not have horizontal water exposure outside the trunk boundary.
- Can use decorators for cave vines, spore blossoms, trunk vines, and cocoa depending on the configured tree variant.

Custom cave-vine/spore-blossom decorators:

- A decorator can choose between doing nothing, placing a spore blossom, or placing cave vines.
- Cave vine length uses an int provider.
- Cave vines stop before non-pass-through blocks or within four blocks of water.
- Cave vines that would already be too close to water are skipped.
- Cave vines generated by these decorators can be configured with berries and age values.

Custom trunk decorators:

- Can cover trunks in vines on multiple sides.
- Jungle variants can place cocoa on lower trunk blocks.
- Some tree variants use trunk decorators only for worldgen, while sapling-grown versions skip them.

Huge fungi / giant fungus:

- Vanilla giant crimson and warped fungus behavior is replaced or supplemented by template-based configured features.
- Crimson fungus has a worldgen version and a from-fungus version, with separate handling for weeping vines.
- Warped fungus has configured feature variants as well.
- Fungi grown by bonemeal or random tick use from-fungus configured features.
- Worldgen uses worldgen configured features.
- Huge fungus placement has stricter air/replaceability checks so trees do not overlap too tightly.
- For 5x5 huge fungus templates, the top four blocks of the final tree require a 7x7 air/replaceable area centered on the trunk to maintain one block of air between neighboring fungus canopies.
- Shroomlights in the source template are intended to remain shroomlights rather than being replaced by wart blocks.

Huge mushrooms:

- Brown and red giant mushroom placement checks are modified.
- Brown mushrooms require enough air below the top canopy so one brown mushroom cannot grow directly under another.
- Huge mushroom validity checks go higher than vanilla without changing the radius passed into the canopy-radius method.

Sugar cane feature:

- Places sugar cane using custom river/shoreline logic tied to chunk-X river bands.
- Some placements are center-adjusted because related jungle/coral features changed from center to corner-based positioning.

Coral and seagrass features:

- Custom coral placement supports underwater tree-ring behavior.
- Coral bonemeal spreading is supported separately through event logic.
- Custom crimson/warped seagrass and kelp features support Nether Depths Upgrade-style underwater/lava vegetation.

  - Mountains/mushroom caves use variants 0-2.

Boss and elite-style mobs:

9. Mob stat and role balancing
------------------------------

The mod uses event/config code to adjust mob stats and drops by biome/tier. The general design is:

- Common mobs spawn normally.
- Elite mobs are stronger and are associated with structures or harder local encounters.
- Boss mobs are much stronger and usually appear as structure bosses.

Biome difficulty order is intended to increase roughly as:

Jungle < Savanna < Plains / Warped Forest < Desert / Mountains < Deep Dark < Soul Sand Valley < Basalt Deltas

The mod contains stat-balancing code for common/elite/boss-style mobs, including consideration for:

- Health.
- Armor.
- Attack damage.
- Movement speed.
- Ranged attacks.
- Equipment such as axes, swords, bows, armor, and shields.
- Special effects like wither, poison, weakness, or slowness.

Examples:

- Ravagers are simplified into common-mob attackers without vanilla shield-breaking, roaring, or stunning emphasis.
- Wardens are reduced from vanilla boss-tier strength to elite-tier strength for deep dark balance.
- Wither skeletons are treated as high-tier basalt-deltas elite mobs with strong melee and wither effect, but not as full bosses.
- Endermen are modified into common mobs by disabling teleportation and tuning their stats.
- Shulkers are common deep-dark mobs, with reduced duplication/defensive behavior.
- Blazes and magma cubes are tuned as basalt-deltas common mobs.
- Zoglins and creepers are tuned as warped-forest mobs.
- Dread liches, dread knights, and trolls are mountain elite mobs.

MobConfig and MobStatsConfig also configure custom drops, breeding rates, aging rates, food growth, and other biome-dependent mob behavior.


10. Mob AI, targeting, and spawn behavior changes
-------------------------------------------------

The mod changes spawn placements for several entities:

- Turtles spawn on land again, but only near configured river-edge chunk bands.
- Drowned can spawn on land using monster spawn rules.
- Striders spawn on solid ground instead of lava, with special valid-ground checks.
- Axolotls and glow squid use custom water spawn checks.
- Husks, strays, pillagers, zoglins, piglin brutes, shulkers, Ice and Fire mobs, and other mobs receive custom placement rules.
- Husks, strays, and similar mobs are allowed to spawn without needing sky exposure.
- Mooshrooms spawn in the grove biome, only above mycelium.

Targeting and attack behavior:

- The mod blocks or removes many mob targeting goals so mobs do not attack most non-villager/non-piglin/non-exception mobs.
- Additional mixins exist for brain-based mobs such as zoglins and wardens, which may not use normal goal selectors.
- Zoglins are quieted when near other mobs if they are not actually attacking.
- Guardians do not attack squid.
- Pufferfish do not poison nearby entities.
- Endermen no longer teleport.
- Wardens lose their sonic boom ranged attack.
- Ravagers are simplified.
- Shulkers do not duplicate from shulker bullets and have common-mob-style behavior.
- Hoglins and piglins are made more passive in configured contexts.
- Piglins do not hunt hoglins.
- Turtle eggs are protected from some mob AI.
- Bee anger, goat ramming/jumping, and other passive/neutral mob behaviors are modified.

Animal food and breeding behavior:

- Rabbits eat ashen wheat for tempting, breeding, and growth speeding.
- Striders eat ashen wheat.
- Mooshrooms can use mushrooms.
- Several animals get expanded breeding/tempt items through event logic, including rabbits, horses, donkeys, turtles, pigs, cats, ocelots, foxes, wolves, moose, pandas, frogs, and others depending on tags/config.
- Twins/fertility behavior can be modified by MobConfig, allowing failed breeding or multiple children.
- Animal aging rates and food-based growth rates can depend on biome/config.

Other mob lifecycle changes:

- Chicken egg and feather behavior is modified by biome/config.
- Sheep wool regrowth and shearing amounts are modified.
- Bamboo can be sheared/adjusted through custom behavior.
- Mooshroom shearing can be disabled.


11. Mob drops and block drops
-----------------------------

Custom mob drops:

- Mob drops are controlled through MobConfig.
- Drops can vary by biome/region.
- Cooked drops can be substituted when a mob dies on fire.
- Looting can affect configured drops for most monsters, with exclusions where configured.
- Cubozoa drop slime based on biome rules:
  - 1 slime in nether biomes.
  - 0.5 expected slime in deep dark and desert.
  - 0 elsewhere.
- Ancient debris drops netherite scrap rather than itself:
  - 2 scrap in basalt deltas.
  - 1 scrap in other nether biomes.
  - 0.5 expected scrap in deep dark and desert.

Custom block drops:

- Block drops are controlled through BlockConfig.
- Custom drop handling cancels vanilla drops for configured blocks.
- Drops can vary by biome/region.
- Fortune and XP are handled.
- Creative breaking avoids drops.
- Some configured blocks can roll grouped seeds or multiple possible outputs.

Berry drops:

- Sweet berries, soul berries, and blue berries have biome/config-dependent harvest amounts.
- Some worldgen replaces half of sweet berry patches with blue berry patches.
- Berry totals can be multiplied and split so a deterministic half/half-minus-half split goes to blue berries rather than relying on an independent 50% chance.


12. Growth, bonemeal, and block behavior configuration
------------------------------------------------------

Biome-dependent growth:

The mod contains many growth-rate hooks and mixins. Similar files are grouped here:

- Crop growth can depend on biome.
- Sapling growth can depend on biome.
- Grass and mycelium spread can depend on biome.
- Amethyst budding/growth can depend on biome.
- Pointed dripstone growth can depend on biome.
- Mangrove propagule growth can depend on biome.
- Vine growth can depend on biome and is modified to grow downward.
- Turtle egg growth/hatching can depend on biome.
- Sheep wool regrowth and animal aging can depend on biome.
- Chicken eggs/feathers can depend on biome.

Bonemeal behavior:

- Bonemeal use is intercepted through event logic.
- The mod consumes one bonemeal and plays the growth effect once.
- Bonemeal output can be multiplied or modified by biome/config.
- Bonemeal works on many vanilla bonemealable blocks plus custom blocks.
- Bonemeal can grow crops, stems, cacti, sugar cane, nether wart, mushrooms, coral, and custom vegetation depending on conditions.
- Bonemeal can transform or decorate certain floor blocks, including sand/red sand, mycelium, sculk, nylium, soul sediments, and basalt-like blocks.
- Bonemeal can spread coral plants and coral fans of the same type.
- Coral blocks are craftable from combinations of plants/fans of the matching coral color.

Crop and plant placement:

- Ashen wheat grows on soul sand and soul soil instead of farmland.
- Eyeblossoms are placeable on soul sand and soul soil.
- Pale oak saplings are placeable on soul sand and soul soil and do not require light.
- Desert grass/tall grass are placeable on sand.
- Mushrooms survive in daylight in more cases.
- Sugar cane can survive without water through mixin behavior.
- Cactus can be placed next to more blocks and does not hurt entities/items in the same way as vanilla.
- Chorus plants/flowers can grow on sculk.
- Cocoa can attach to any overworld log.
- Spore blossoms can attach under leaves.
- Nether fungi, roots, sprouts, and wart-like vegetation have expanded placement/survival behavior.
- Pumpkin/melon stems do not kill farmland in the vanilla way.

Fire, collision, and light:

- Campfires do not damage entities.
- Sweet berry bushes do not slow or hurt mobs.
- Soul sand, soul soil, and honey no longer slow entities.
- Cave vines are brighter and have modified waterlogging/berry behavior.
- Coral blocks glow.
- Glow moss and related blocks have modified light/no-occlusion behavior.
- Less random block offset is applied to some blocks.
- Sea pickle and ice-on-water behavior are modified.

Double plants:

- A cleanup event removes orphaned upper/lower halves of double plants when worldgen or block placement overwrites the other half.
- This cleanup avoids generating drops.


13. Fire, fluids, and biome safety
----------------------------------

No tropical fire behavior:

- Fire behavior is disabled or canceled in tropical/rainforest-like biomes.
- This includes normal fire placement, soul fire placement, fire damage/burning behavior, and lightning-related fire effects.

Fluid placement/spread:

- Water and lava placement/spread can be blocked or transformed based on biome/context.
- Lava entering tropical/rainforest-style areas can become moss-like blocks.
- Water entering nether-style contexts can become obsidian-like blocks.
- Nether portal fire ignition is disabled through a dimension-related mixin.


14. Recipes and recipe filtering
-------------------------------

The mod adds many crafting, smelting, smoking, campfire cooking, food, tool, coral, redstone, and integration recipes.

Cooking recipes include:

- Modded fish and cooked fish.
- End fish, glow squid, pufferfish, tropical fish, lobster, Naturalist venison, and prickly pear.
- Repurposed Drop The Meat items where used by the current food setup.
- Naturalist venison cooking in furnace, smoker, and campfire variants.

Food crafting includes:

- Pies such as melon pie, glow berry pie, banana pie, mango pie, pear pie, plum pie, and cherry pie.
- Cookies such as soul berry cookie and blue berry cookie.
- Breads such as ashen bread.
- Wraps/rolls such as searing cod roll and nether fish kelp roll.
- Integration soups/stews such as Nourished Nether nether fungus stew and Better Carrots carrot soup where present.

Coral recipes:

- Each coral block can be crafted from four coral plants/fans of the same coral color in any combination.

Copper automation/redstone recipes:

- Redstone/automation components involving iron can accept copper ingots as an alternative unless the item has iron in its name or is intentionally excluded.
- This includes vanilla automation components such as hoppers and selected Quark components.
- Quark recipes are whitelisted so Quark integration recipes remain available.
- Ender watcher, feeding trough, and gravisand are intentionally excluded from copper alternatives.

Netherite recipes:

- Netherite ingot is made by smelting netherite scrap.
- Netherite armor/tools/weapons are crafted from netherite ingots directly.
- Vanilla smithing-upgrade recipes are overridden/disabled so netherite is not applied to diamond equipment in the normal way.

Recipe namespace filtering:

- A mixin removes recipes from namespaces other than minecraft, aoemod, and quark.

Important audit note:

- This is intentionally broad. Any non-AOE, non-Minecraft, non-Quark mod recipe will be removed unless separately whitelisted or recreated under an allowed namespace.

20. Audit checklist / things worth double-checking
--------------------------------------------------

These are behaviors that are especially broad or easy to accidentally leave in an unwanted state.

1. Overworld replacement:

   The mod overrides data/minecraft/dimension/overworld.json. If this is not intended for every world using the mod, it is the biggest global behavior change.

2. Recipe namespace filtering:

   Recipes outside minecraft, aoemod, and quark are removed. This can silently disable recipes from almost every other installed mod unless recreated or whitelisted.

3. Mixin list duplicates:

   aoemod.mixins.json includes similarly named glowing-moss spawn mixins and several broad behavior mixins. Duplicate or overlapping mixins should be reviewed if spawn-surface behavior acts strangely.

4. External mod assumptions:

   Java and JSON reference several external mods. If one is missing and not treated as required/optional correctly in mods.toml, startup or datapack loading can fail.

5. Custom drops replacing vanilla drops:

   Blocks and mobs configured in BlockConfig/MobConfig can have vanilla drops canceled. If a block/mob gives no drops, check whether it is configured but has no matching biome entry.

6. Tree and fungus template behavior:

   Template blocks such as shroomlights, structure voids, logs, and leaves are replaced by provider logic. If a template block is unexpectedly converted, check the provider-replacement conditions.

7. Mob target filtering:

   Goal-selector and brain-based AI patches may prevent some mobs from attacking things they normally would. If a mob seems passive, check both the normal AI removal mixins and brain-related mixins.

8. Netherite balance:

   Netherite gear intentionally has iron-like stats. If netherite feels weak, this is from the mod's item/tier mixins and recipes, not vanilla.
